\begin{abstract}
This mini-report describes a lightweight traffic monitoring system using computer vision. It detects vehicles, counts them, estimates density, and identifies congestion from video feeds. YOLOv8 and OpenCV are used for detection and tracking. Preliminary results show good accuracy in urban Indian scenarios.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Traffic congestion is a major issue in cities like Bengaluru \citep{bangalore-traffic-2025}. Traditional methods (loops, manual counting) are expensive and inaccurate.

Computer vision provides a camera-based, scalable solution \citep{opencv-book}. The proposed system:
\begin{itemize}
\item Detects and classifies vehicles in real time
\item Tracks vehicles across frames
\item Computes density and congestion levels
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Detection and Tracking}
YOLOv8 nano model (pre-trained on COCO, fine-tuned on Indian traffic data) handles detection \citep{yolov8-paper}. ByteTrack is used for multi-object tracking.
